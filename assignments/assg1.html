# Assignment: Heuristic Evaluation

# Details

You will work in pairs to do a heuristic evaluation on an existing application of your choice.

# Instructions

Select an app that you have used a fair bit, and which you find difficult or unpleasant to use. Select something that has enough content to find 8 things wrong with it.

ðŸ”” If you choose to work alone, you are expected to find 4 things wrong with your app.

In this assignment, you will go beyond that initial gut reaction of not liking an app, to articulate in an objective way what the app's main problems are, and how they relate to the heuristics described in the lecture/readings.

  • You are expected to find at least 8 elements which either respect, or violate the usability heuristics outlined in this lecture. You will document them in the template provided below, noting:

    • the feature/part of the application
    • describe the problem (what's wrong)
    • what heuristic you're referring to (pick whichever works best, or two if it relates to two)
    • the severity (see the table in the document)
    • make a recommendation on how to make it better
    • include a screenshot

  • Don't worry about making a pretty document - focus on providing a truly objective evaluation of the app, as they relate to the 10 usability heuristics.

  • You may have multiple findings for one feature, or have to point to a different feature for each finding you document.

  • Follow the attached example file to capture your findings: heuristicEvaluation.docx

# Deliverable for this assignment:

  • Follow the document example to complete your work for submission
  • One submission per group: Include each group memeber name to their respective writing section
  • Filename: firstNameLastName_heuristicEvaluation.docx

Note

  • Bugs are not usability issues
  • Feature requests are not usability issues
  • Business decisions (usually) are not usability issues (ex. The fact that you have to wait for an ad to play.)
