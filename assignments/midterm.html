# Midterm: Presenting your concepts

# Details

For your midterm, you will prepare and present concepts for an app for Doors Open Ottawa.

# Instructions

  • There is no submission for this assignment - it is delivered as an in-class presentation. (20% of total grade)
  • Presentations must be done in-class, in Week 07, on February 23 during your class.
    • Each student will have 5 minutes to present their wireframes of their Doors Open Ottawa app concept. (The wireframes are prepared during your week 5/6 exercise)
    • 3 minutes to present
    • 2 minutes of critique (be prepared to answer some questions)
    • If presenting in class, use the computer and projector from the teacher's desk.

# You will be graded on 3 aspects:

  1. 5 points for the presentation
  • did you speak loudly, clearly, and look at the audience?
  • was your presentation well-prepared and rehearsed?
  • did you use the time well? (was it too short or too long?)
  • did you justify your decisions, and answer questions well?
  1. 5 points for the content
  • did you show all the required parts of the concept?
    • main screen
    • navigation
    • 1-2 sub-screens (depending on depth of hierarchy)
    • necessary screens to show moving back/between screens
      • Example: home > list of buildings > buidling details > browse a few photos > home
  • were your wireframes clear and easy to understand?
  • did they have real/realistic content?
  1. 5 points for the concept proposed
  • did you follow good UI design principles taught so far in class?
  • did you choose and use navigation models appropriately?
  • did you follow the platform design guidelines?

# So… before that class, you need to:

  • Continue refining/evolving the wireframes you made in class in week 5/6, to develop a complete concept
    • Use the tool of your choice (Adeobe XD or Figma etc.)
  • Decide how you plan to present
    • Slide deck, accompanied by you speaking?
    • Multimedia/animated/prototype, with you speaking?
    • Live sketching on the whiteboard? (Watch the time! It's tight!)
  • Prepare a 3-minute presentation, making sure you cover the important points of your design that you want feedback on. (You won’t have time to show everything, so plan well and be efficient.)
    • Walk through the app, showing a realistic use scenario
    • Make sure you have the necessary screens to explain the flow through the app concept
  • Practice your presentation
    • Adjust it as needed - the wireframes, slides, and presentation

# Deliverable for this assignment:

  • In-class presentations in Week 07: Feb 23 at 9am for section 02 and at 5pm for section 01
  • Optional: Upload your presentation slides to the Assignments page
